Basic Mechanics

The order of the videos showcased are in this order: simple spawn and destory, simple sprite display and animation, projectiles, and enemies on a 2D environment, raycasting and affecting said raycast, using raycasting to pick up and drop items, turning on and off a mesh renderer using a key, and adding simple footsteps audio.

Treat Collection

A game with a theme about dogs featuring my dog as the background for the game, as well as music made by me, and it is about a dog who has an insatiable hunger for dog treats, and everytime he gets full, the dog gets more hungry the next time.

I used javascript to code the whole game including the character, treats, and changing state machine. I also made the music for each state of the game using a piano, and I used pictures of my dog to keep the dog themed game going.

First 2D Project

First 2D project made in Unity. A space invaders like game where there are random enemies spawning as well as asteroids, and the goal is to survive and score as high as possible. The character is on the bottom and can only move left and right, but it can shoot to gain score, and he has 3 lives before it is game over.

I scripted all the player and enemy movements. The player can use a and d to move as well as space for shooting projectiles and B for shooting bombs. I scripted randomized patterns and fire rate as well for both the player and the enemies.

Coding Challenge #1 Spring Semester

What was asked to be included: an object controlled by the player that is confined to the bottom half of the screen. This means that when the player is moving up they are stopped at the middle of the screen. When moving down they are stopped at the bottom of the screen. On either side the player's movement should wrap to the other side. So I the player moves past the edge of the scene to the right , they reappear on the left. Multiple objects that moves on their own from the top of the screen to the bottom and wraps around (reappears at the top of the screen)

Coding Challenge #2 Spring Semester

Expectations: Add Shooting and enemies to your Movement Demo environment. The additions need to demonstrate an understanding of how to get:

  • Spawning of Projectiles. The projectile needs to:
  • move in the pointed direction
  • need to disappear when off the screen
  • needs to disappear when it hits a target (note is may bounce off other objects that aren't targets)
  • spawning and moving of Obstacles/enemies in random patterns
  • select obstacles/enemies can be destroyed by any collision (projectile or player.
  • obstacle/enemy firing projectile
  • player destroyed on collision with obstacle
